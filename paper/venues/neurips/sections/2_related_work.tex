\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

\paragraph{Context-conditioned and meta-reinforcement learning.}
Meta-RL and context-conditioned control address hidden task or dynamics variation by learning policies that adapt from experience. Gradient-based meta-learning and learned dynamics adaptation can tune policies or models after observing a small amount of data~\cite{finn2017model,nagabandi2019learning}, while latent-context methods infer a task embedding from recent transitions and condition the policy on that embedding~\cite{rakelly2019efficient,zintgraf2021varibad}. \PTA{} uses this latter idea in a deliberately ordinary form: a short trace is encoded into a latent context, and a residual policy conditions on that latent. The difference is not a new inference architecture. Instead, we separate the data used to infer context from the data produced by the task rollout, then evaluate a complication often hidden by standard adaptation benchmarks: in contact-rich materials, the experience used for inference is also an intervention that changes the state distribution.

\paragraph{Active system identification and information-gathering control.}
Online adaptation has also been studied as system identification, where policies or models infer low-dimensional dynamics parameters during deployment. Model-based meta-learning and online adaptation methods update dynamics estimates from recent interaction~\cite{nagabandi2018deep,clavera2019modelbased}, and sim-to-real pipelines combine broad training variation with online estimation of physical parameters or actuator properties~\cite{yu2017preparing,peng2018sim2real}. Recent manipulation systems make identification explicit through active exploration for simulator refinement~\cite{memmel2024asid}, material-adaptive deformable dynamics~\cite{zhang2024adaptigraph}, or elastoplastic parameter estimation~\cite{yang2025differentiable}. These approaches target better estimates; \PTA{} uses a fixed open-loop probe and standard encoder/controller to isolate whether context gain outweighs state disturbance.

\paragraph{Embodied probing and physical exploration.}
Robotics has long treated perception as an active process in which actions reveal hidden physical information. Early active vision~\cite{bajcsy1988active} and haptic exploratory procedures~\cite{lederman1987hand} established that contact motions can be designed around the property to be sensed. Modern tactile manipulation uses exploratory contact to estimate geometry, contact conditions, affordances, pose, and physical properties~\cite{calandra2018more,xu2013tactile,bauza2020tactile,yu2016more,bauza2018data,dutta2025predictive}, while tactile-informed dynamics models fold contact observations into dense non-prehensile manipulation~\cite{ai2024robopack}. This literature motivates probing as measurement, but many settings involve rigid objects, local contact properties, or closed-loop controllers where downstream damage is not isolated. Our setting is less benign: the same push that reveals stiffness, cohesion, or plasticity can leave grooves, compact material, or alter the target configuration. We make the recoverability of that disturbance the diagnostic hypothesis.

\paragraph{Deformable-material benchmarks as hidden-dynamics stress tests.}
Recent simulators and benchmarks make it practical to evaluate control under diverse soft-body and granular dynamics. Genesis supports unified rigid, MPM, SPH, and FEM simulation~\cite{genesis2024}; PlasticineLab and DiffSkill study differentiable MPM skill learning~\cite{huang2022plasticinelab,lin2022diffskill}; RoboCraft and RoboCook examine deformable tool use~\cite{shi2022robocraft,shi2023robocook}; GranularGym targets high-throughput granular tasks~\cite{millard2023granulargym}; and SoftGym spans cloth, rope, and fluid-like domains~\cite{lin2021softgym}. These benchmarks emphasize deformable control, planning, or differentiable simulation. Our benchmark instead fixes one edge-pushing task and varies hidden material regimes to stress-test context acquisition under controlled train/test splits. This yields a diagnostic sign pattern: explicit probing improves the recoverable elastoplastic split and is worse on four persistent-perturbation splits. Our novelty claim is the controlled evaluation of a minimal active-context-acquisition mechanism where information gathering can alter the task state.
